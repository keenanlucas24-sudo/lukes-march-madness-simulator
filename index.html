7-Factor Simulation Model

1. Efficiency Matchup

Not just "who is better" — each team's offense is evaluated against the specific opponent's defense. A 130 AdjOE vs. a 90 AdjDE plays very differently than vs. a 100 AdjDE. Uses KenPom adjusted efficiency data.

2. Tempo Mismatch

Harvard Sports Analysis found tempo manipulation drives upsets. UMBC sped Virginia up; FDU slowed Purdue down. When teams are forced out of their preferred pace, variance spikes. Low-tempo games are inherently more random.

3. Three-Point Variance

The great equalizer. Game-to-game 3PT% has ~8.5% standard deviation. A 35% shooter can go 2/16 or 8/16 any night. Teams with high 3PT rates (Alabama: 48%) are explosive but volatile. Each game simulates actual shooting variance.

4. Free Throw Clutch

Champions shoot 71.9% FT vs. 70.1% field average. In close tournament games, FT shooting decides outcomes. The model simulates actual FT attempts and makes per game, with higher impact in tight contests.

5. Experience Factor

Veteran rosters handle tournament pressure better. First-time tournament programs get a "bright lights" penalty. Roster continuity and program tournament history both factor into composure under pressure.

6. Consistency / Volatility

Rock-solid teams (Michigan: 0.90) rarely get upset but rarely upset others. Volatile teams (Alabama: 0.55) can beat anyone or lose to anyone. Inconsistency increases variance in both directions.

7. Depth & Fatigue

Deep benches matter more each round as games come every 2 days. Thin benches fade late. Gonzaga's 42% bench minutes vs. Texas Tech's 19% becomes a massive factor by the Elite 8.
