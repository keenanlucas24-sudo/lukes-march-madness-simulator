11-Factor Simulation Model

Each game simulates all 11 factors with stochastic variance, then generates a narrative explaining the outcome. The model is calibrated against 40 years of historical upset rates and blended with live Vegas market data.

1

Efficiency Matchup

Each team's offense is evaluated against the specific opponent's defense using KenPom adjusted efficiency data. Margins are compressed 45% for tournament regression (single-elimination, neutral courts).

2

Tempo Mismatch

Teams forced out of their preferred pace see higher variance. UMBC sped Virginia up; FDU slowed Purdue down. Low-tempo games are inherently more random — fewer possessions = higher variance per possession.

3

3-Point Variance

The great equalizer. Game-to-game 3PT% has ~9% standard deviation. Each game simulates actual shooting variance. High 3PT-rate teams (Alabama: 48%) are explosive but volatile.

4

Free Throw Clutch

Champions shoot 71.9% FT vs. 70.1% field average. The model simulates actual FT attempts per game — crucial in close contests that tournament games tend to be.

5

Experience & Composure

Veteran rosters handle tournament pressure better. First-time tournament programs get a "bright lights" penalty. Both roster age and program tournament history factor in.

6

Momentum

Teams entering the tournament on a hot streak carry that energy forward. Conversely, teams limping in with losses face a confidence deficit under single-elimination pressure.

7

Depth & Fatigue

Deep benches matter more each round as games come every 2 days. Gonzaga's 42% bench minutes vs. Texas Tech's 19% becomes a massive factor by the Elite 8.

8

Coaching Pedigree

Elite tournament coaches (Izzo, Pitino, Hurley, Self) compress variance through superior preparation and in-game adjustments. Each coach rated 0-5 based on career tournament record, Final Fours, and championships. Impact scales up in later rounds.

9

Injury / Availability

Missing key players directly reduces team effectiveness. Alabama's Holloway suspension (16.8 PPG), UNC's Wilson OUT (19.8 PPG), and Gonzaga's Huff OUT (17.8 PPG) are reflected in real-time impact ratings.

10

Conference Strength

Big 12 and Big East have produced 4 of the last 5 champions. Teams from power conferences get a premium; weak conference teams face a discount. Based on NET rankings and recent championship production.

11

Vegas Market Calibration

A 15% blend toward DraftKings/BetMGM market lines anchors the model to collective market wisdom — millions of dollars of information from sharp bettors, computer models, and public sentiment.
