### Per-Champion Profiles

#### 2010 — Duke Blue Devils (35-5) | Seed: 1 | ACC
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #1 AdjO, #5 AdjD
- **Coach:** Mike Krzyzewski (4th NCAA title; career titles: 4 at the time)
- **Key players:** Kyle Singler, Jon Scheyer, Nolan Smith, Mason Plumlee
- **Style:** Half-court, deliberate offense; efficient two-point scoring; elite free-throw shooting (ACC Tournament: 24-of-28 FTs vs. Georgia Tech)
- **Narrative:** Duke survived a scare from 5-seed Butler in the championship game, winning 61-59. Singler led the offense and Scheyer provided veteran leadership. The Dukies were the consensus best team in the country entering the tournament, and their KenPom profile reflected a perfectly balanced attack. The championship was won on a Gordon Hayward half-court heave that rimmed out.
- **Tournament seed:** #1 overall seed in the South region

#### 2011 — Connecticut Huskies (32-9) | Seed: 3 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #10 overall, #19 AdjO, #15 AdjD
- **Coach:** Jim Calhoun (3rd NCAA title)
- **Key players:** Kemba Walker (MOP), Jeremy Lamb, Alex Oriakhi
- **Style:** Guard-driven, Kemba Walker one-man wrecking crew; won 11 straight postseason games including 5 in the Big East Tournament
- **Narrative:** The most improbable champion of the modern era based on KenPom. UConn was the 9th seed in their own conference tournament, entered the NCAA tournament ranked #21 nationally, and had lost 4 of their last 5 games before the Big East tournament. Kemba Walker's Herculean run—carrying his team through 5 Big East games then 6 NCAA games—is unmatched. They beat Butler (8-seed) 53-41 in a low-scoring final. The team's overall KenPom of #10 remains the second-lowest for any champion (2002-present).
- **Fun stat:** Beat 2-seed San Diego State, 1-seed Arizona, 1-seed Ohio State (via Kentucky in SF), and 8-seed Butler on their run.

#### 2012 — Kentucky Wildcats (38-2) | Seed: 1 | SEC
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #2 AdjO, #7 AdjD (per Reddit KenPom data)
- **Coach:** John Calipari (1st NCAA title)
- **Key players:** Anthony Davis (MOP; 14 pts, 16 reb in title game; 4.1 BPG season), Michael Kidd-Gilchrist, Marquis Teague, Doron Lamb
- **Style:** Elite athleticism, rim protection via Davis (333 team blocks, NCAA record at time), fast-paced, dominant interior
- **Statistical profile:** Won all 6 tournament games by 8+ points; FT%: 71% in title game (15-of-21)
- **Narrative:** One of the most dominant tournament runs ever. Anthony Davis, a freshman, averaged 14.4 ppg / 10.4 rpg / 4.7 bpg en route to the Naismith Player of the Year award. Kentucky's lone loss of the season was at Indiana (buzzer-beater); they avenged it in the Sweet 16. The team was stacked with future NBA lottery picks. By any metric, arguably the deepest roster of any champion in this era.

#### 2013 — Louisville Cardinals (35-5)* | Seed: 1 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #7 AdjO, #1 AdjD
- **Coach:** Rick Pitino (2nd title; 1st vacated)
- **Key players:** Russ Smith, Luke Hancock (MOP), Peyton Siva, Gorgui Dieng
- **Conference:** Big East (final year before conference realignment)
- **Style:** Elite defense (#1 nationally), press-and-run; opponents held to some of the lowest point totals in the bracket
- **Narrative:** Louisville was built on defense—rated #1 nationally in KenPom's adjusted defensive efficiency—and used it to grind through a Big East bracket in their last year in the conference. They defeated #8 seed Michigan in the final 82-76. Luke Hancock won MOP after coming off the bench. The title was vacated in 2018 following an NCAA investigation into recruiting violations (sex scandal at the program).

#### 2014 — UConn Huskies (32-8) | Seed: 7 | AAC
- **KenPom:** #15 overall, #39 AdjO (!), #10 AdjD
- **Coach:** Kevin Ollie (1st title, 2nd year as head coach)
- **Key players:** Shabazz Napier (MOP; 22 pts in final), Ryan Boatright, Niels Giffey
- **Style:** Defense-first, opportunistic offense; Napier carried the scoring load
- **Narrative:** The single greatest "miracle" run in modern March Madness history from a statistical standpoint. UConn ranked #39 in adjusted offensive efficiency—the lowest of any champion in the KenPom era by a massive margin. As a 7-seed, they became the first (and still only) 7-seed to win a national title. They faced the highest combined seed total (7 + 8 = 15) in championship game history (vs. Kentucky). They defeated top programs: #4 Iowa State, #2 Villanova, #1 Florida, #3 Michigan State, and #8 Kentucky. Napier had an iconic tournament, surviving a near-miss in the Round of 64 against St. Joseph's thanks to a controversial "and-one" call in the final minute.

#### 2015 — Duke Blue Devils (35-4) | Seed: 1 | ACC
- **KenPom:** #3 overall, #3 AdjO, #11 AdjD
- **Coach:** Mike Krzyzewski (5th and final title)
- **Key players:** Tyus Jones (MOP), Jahlil Okafor, Justise Winslow, Grayson Allen
- **Style:** Talented freshman-heavy roster (three lottery picks), versatile offense, enough defense to get by
- **Narrative:** Duke benefited from Wisconsin taking out heavily-favored Kentucky (undefeated entering tournament) in the national semi-final. Coach K's last championship was fueled by a remarkably talented freshman class. Duke trailed Wisconsin 58-56 in the final before Jones hit a go-ahead layup and-one to seal the win. The team was heavily reliant on Okafor in the paint and Jones orchestrating; an unusual, offense-first Duke team.

#### 2016 — Villanova Wildcats (35-5) | Seed: 2 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #3 AdjO, #5 AdjD
- **Coach:** Jay Wright (1st title)
- **Key players:** Kris Jenkins (buzzer-beater champion), Josh Hart, Ryan Arcidiacono, Jalen Brunson (freshman)
- **Style:** Ball-movement, motion offense, disciplined defense; high 3PT% team
- **Narrative:** Won on one of the most iconic shots in sports history—Kris Jenkins' three-pointer at the buzzer to beat North Carolina 77-74. Villanova was a perfectly constructed team: great ball movement, clutch veterans, and a system that maximized every player's role. Jay Wright had built a program culture that prioritized unselfishness and team chemistry. The shot came after Marcus Paige hit a miraculous double-pump three to tie it with 4.7 seconds left.

#### 2017 — North Carolina Tar Heels (33-7) | Seed: 1 | ACC
- **KenPom:** #3 overall, #9 AdjO, #11 AdjD
- **Coach:** Roy Williams (3rd title)
- **Key players:** Joel Berry II (MOP), Justin Jackson, Kennedy Meeks, Isaiah Hicks
- **Style:** Uptempo, inside-out attack; led nation in pace; physical big men combined with perimeter shooting
- **Narrative:** North Carolina returned from the heartbreak of the 2016 Villanova buzzer-beater to win the title they felt they had been denied. They survived a 7-seed South Carolina upset in the Sweet 16 round and beat Gonzaga 71-65 in the final. Roy Williams' sixth national title (3 with UNC), combining experience (Berry was a sophomore making good on the prior year's stumble) with athleticism.

#### 2018 — Villanova Wildcats (36-4) | Seed: 1 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #1 AdjO, #11 AdjD
- **Coach:** Jay Wright (2nd title)
- **Key players:** Donte DiVincenzo (MOP, 31 pts off bench in final), Jalen Brunson, Mikal Bridges, Phil Booth
- **Style:** Elite offense (#1 nationally), precise ball movement, three-point shooting, role acceptance
- **Statistical profile:** Villanova dominated the entire 2018 tournament, winning every game by double digits (17.75 avg margin). FT%: strong throughout; 3PT%: one of the best 3PT shooting teams in the country
- **Narrative:** Jay Wright's masterpiece. This Villanova team was arguably the best pure offensive outfit of the era, finishing #1 in KenPom adjusted offensive efficiency. DiVincenzo exploding for 31 off the bench in the final against Michigan was one of the tournament's iconic individual performances. The team won every NCAA game by at least 9 points—a staggering display of sustained dominance. Two titles in three years cemented Wright as an all-time program builder.

#### 2019 — Virginia Cavaliers (35-3) | Seed: 1 | ACC
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #2 AdjO, #5 AdjD
- **Coach:** Tony Bennett (1st title)
- **Key players:** Kyle Guy (MOP), Ty Jerome, Braxton Key, De'Andre Hunter
- **Style:** Pack Line Defense (suffocating, zone-adjacent man-to-man), deliberate half-court offense; slowest tempo of any champion in the era
- **Narrative:** One year after becoming the first #1 seed to ever lose to a #16 (UMBC, 2018), Virginia returned, entered as the top overall seed, and won the championship in overtime (85-77 vs. Texas Tech). The Pack Line defense—Virginia's signature system—led the nation in limiting points per possession for years. Kyle Guy went 4-of-4 from the free throw line in the final seconds of regulation and overtime to seal the win. A remarkable redemption story and the third straight #1 seed to win a title (following Villanova 2018 and North Carolina 2017).

#### 2021 — Baylor Bears (28-2) | Seed: 1 | Big 12
- **KenPom:** #2 overall, #3 AdjO, #44 AdjD (defense fell off late in year from #1 early in season)
- **Coach:** Scott Drew (1st title; 18-year rebuild from NCAA sanctions)
- **Key players:** Jared Butler (16.7 ppg), Davion Mitchell (Naismith Defensive Player of the Year), MaCio Teague (15.9 ppg)
- **Statistical profile:** Led nation in 3PT% (41.3%); 5th in offensive rebounding rate (37.3%); forced turnovers on 24.4% of opponent possessions; 3-point shooting: Butler .416, Mitchell .447, Flagler .434
- **FT%:** Butler .780, Teague .831 — strong FT shooters across the board
- **Tempo:** KenPom rank ~179 (below average pace)
- **Narrative:** Scott Drew took over a program in 2003 under a cloud of NCAA violations, banned from postseason play, with nearly every scholarship player transferring. 18 years later, he steamrolled undefeated Gonzaga 86-70 in the title game. Baylor led the nation in 3-point shooting percentage and played elite on-ball defense through Davion Mitchell (the "Off Night" Mitchell was so disruptive he shut down Gonzaga's offense). A season-long #1 or #2 team finally delivered when it mattered most.

#### 2022 — Kansas Jayhawks (34-6) | Seed: 1 | Big 12
- **KenPom:** #3 (efficiency margin), #6 AdjO, #29 AdjD
- **Coach:** Bill Self (2nd title)
- **Key players:** Ochai Agbaji (MOP), Remy Martin (critical role player), David McCormack, Jalen Wilson
- **Narrative:** Kansas trailed North Carolina by 16 points at halftime in the championship game—the largest deficit overcome in title game history. Agbaji led the comeback and Remy Martin, a transfer guard, was the spark off the bench. Bill Self finally earned his second ring after multiple deep tournament runs. UNC was an 8-seed in the Final Four having beaten defending champion Baylor (1-seed) earlier in the bracket.
- **Conference:** Big 12 (Self's dominant home)
- **Score:** 72-69

#### 2023 — UConn Huskies (31-8) | Seed: 4 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #4 overall, #6 AdjO, #18 AdjD
- **Coach:** Dan Hurley (1st title)
- **Key players:** Adama Sanogo (MOP), Jordan Hawkins (38.8% 3PT), Tristen Newton, Donovan Clingan
- **Statistical profile:** 3PT%: 38.8% (team); FT%: 81.6% (team); defensive rebounding strong; allowed only 63.4 PPG
- **Narrative:** UConn beat San Diego State (5-seed, defensive specialist) 76-59 in a game that was never close after the first half. Dan Hurley's first title came in a tournament that featured one of the biggest upsets ever (FDU over Purdue, 16-over-1). UConn was the highest seed (4) to win since Florida in 2006 (also a 3-seed). They weren't a dominant regular season team (31-8 with a 4th-place Big East finish) but turned on the jets in March.

#### 2024 — UConn Huskies (37-3) | Seed: 1 | Big East
- **KenPom:** #1 overall, #1 AdjO, #11 AdjD
- **Coach:** Dan Hurley (2nd consecutive title)
- **Key players:** Tristen Newton (MOP; 20 pts, 7 ast, 5 reb in final), Donovan Clingan, Cam Spencer, Andre Jackson Jr.
- **Statistical profile:** Won every tournament game by 14+ points; avg margin of victory: 23.3 points in 2024 tournament; 3PT%: 35.8%; FT%: 74.3%
- **Tempo:** KenPom rank #329 (among the slowest teams in the nation — methodical, possessions-per-game minimized)
- **Narrative:** One of the most dominant two-year NCAA Tournament runs in history. UConn crushed Purdue 75-60 in the final, winning by 15 points in a game that felt lopsided throughout. The 2024 UConn team was the #1 overall seed and backed it up entirely—no close calls, no stress. Clingan's interior presence and Newton's point guard playmaking were the foundation of a team that peaked at exactly the right time.

#### 2025 — Florida Gators (36-4) | Seed: 1 | SEC
- **KenPom:** #2 overall (entering tournament), #1 AdjO, #10 AdjD, #59 tempo rank
- **Coach:** Todd Golden (1st title; 3rd year at Florida)
- **Key players:** Walter Clayton Jr., Will Richard, Alex Condon
- **Score:** 65-63 over Houston
- **Narrative:** Florida survived a tight final over 1-seed Houston (65-63) to claim their first national title since 2007. Todd Golden, only 37 years old at the time of the championship, became one of the youngest coaches to ever win an NCAA title. Florida's KenPom profile—#1 in offensive efficiency with a top-10 defense—fit the prototypical champion mold exactly. The 2025 tournament's Final Four was historically chalk: all four 1-seeds advanced (only the second time ever).

### Cinderella Statistical DNA: Cross-Era Patterns

Based on analysis from Berkeley Sports Analytics, Fox Sports research, and VSiN historical data:

#### Confirmed Cinderella Traits (statistically validated):

1. **Adjusted Efficiency Margin:** Cinderella teams (Sweet 16+ as 10-seed or lower) average nearly **double the Net Rating** of non-Cinderella underdog teams. They are genuinely good teams misranked by their seed.

2. **Defense:** Cinderella teams show a **significantly lower (better) Defensive Rating** than non-Cinderella underdog peers. Elite defense is the single most reliable Cinderella predictor. Average KenPom defensive efficiency rank for Cinderella mid-majors reaching Sweet 16: **~79** (top quarter nationally).

3. **Tempo:** Cinderella teams overwhelmingly **play slower than average** (outside top 100 in adjusted tempo). Slowing the game down limits possessions, which limits the higher seed's scoring opportunities. NC State 2024, Loyola Chicago 2018, Saint Peter's 2022, Princeton 2023, VCU 2011 (paradoxically fast), and Oral Roberts 2021 (fast) are the outliers in different directions.

4. **3PT%:** For mid-major Cinderellas specifically, **above 35% from three** is a key threshold. Oral Roberts (elite 3PT), Yale, Loyola Chicago, Princeton all shot efficiently from three. Cinderella teams as a whole don't necessarily shoot *more* threes than peers, but their **make rate is higher** when they take them.

5. **Experience:** Cinderella rosters tend to be **older (more upperclassmen)** than average. VCU 2011 had the 15th-oldest roster. Loyola 2018 was veteran-heavy. Saint Peter's 2022 had battle-hardened mid-major players. Experience translates to composure in close games.

6. **Free throw shooting:** Saint Peter's 2022 specifically weaponized the free throw line (18-of-21 vs. Kentucky), and Cinderella teams that get to the line and convert (FT% > 72%) show higher success rates in close games.

7. **Mid-range shooting:** Surprisingly, successful Cinderellas show **higher mid-range shot frequency and accuracy** than non-Cinderellas, suggesting a balanced, multi-option offensive identity.

8. **Offensive rebounding:** Above-average offensive rebounding rate (second-chance points) is a key differentiator for Cinderella teams.

9. **Strength of schedule:** Mid-major Cinderellas that played **tougher non-conference schedules** are better prepared for tournament competition.

#### Failed Cinderella Archetype:
- Teams that rely on **fast transition and rim-attacking** (without shooting threat) — statistically the least successful archetype.
- Teams with **high turnover rates** (>11 TO/game) rarely sustain Cinderella runs regardless of talent level.

#### The VCU 2011 Exception:
VCU was a true statistical outlier — they played **fast** (not slow), used a full-court pressure defense (HAVOC), and generated forced turnovers at a historic rate. Their approach was the opposite of most Cinderellas but worked because the HAVOC defense neutralized more talented opponents by stripping possessions from them entirely. They also had an old roster (15th oldest) and a high KenPom AdjEM ranking that their 11-seed belied.

### Key Thresholds (from KenPom research):

### Offense vs. Defense: Which Wins Championships?

Based on the data, **offense edges defense as the primary predictor** — but both must be strong:

- Only **6 of 18 champions** (2002-2020) had a lower (better) AdjO rank than their AdjD rank — meaning most champions were *more* elite offensively than defensively.
- The average champion ranked **8.25 in AdjO vs. 16.33 in AdjD** — offense is ranked roughly twice as well as defense on average.
- However, only **3 champions** finished outside the top 10 in AdjO (2014 UConn #39, 2011 UConn #19, 2017 UNC #9), while **7 were outside the top 10 in AdjD** — suggesting elite offense is *more essential* but that good (not necessarily elite) defense is acceptable.
- The "Championship Mold" (per 247Sports): **Top 25 in both AdjO and AdjD** — 22 of last 23 champions met this standard.

## SECTION 6: SIMULATION MODEL CALIBRATION WEIGHTS

### Key Findings for Simulation Use

#### Champion Prediction Rules (from KenPom historical data):
1. **Top 25 in both AdjO and AdjD** → 95%+ of all champions met this standard; flag teams outside this box as major underdogs
2. **Top 6 in KenPom overall** → 26 of 28 champions (93%) were in the top 6 entering the tournament
3. **1-seeds win ~65% of all titles** (26/40); 2-seeds win ~12.5%; 3-seeds win ~10%
4. **No champion has entered the tournament outside the top 25 of adjusted efficiency margin** in the KenPom era (since 2002), except 2014 UConn (#15 overall but outlier due to Shabazz Napier peak performance)

#### Round-by-Round Upset Calibration:
- **R64 (1 vs. 16):** Set upset probability at 1.2% — very rare; only happens ~once per decade
- **R64 (2 vs. 15):** Set at 6.9%; trending upward (2021, 2022, 2023 were 3 straight years)
- **R64 (5 vs. 12):** Set at 35.6%; happening in 34 of 40 tournaments (at least one per year almost always)
- **R64 (6 vs. 11):** Set at 38.7% and rising (11-seeds won more than 6-seeds from 2011-2025)
- **R64 (8 vs. 9):** Set at ~50/50; 9-seeds have winning record all-time
- **R32 (4 vs. 5 effective matchup):** Near coin flip (54.9% for 4-seed)
- **Elite 8 (1 vs. 2):** Nearly 50/50 (27-24 all-time for 1-seed)
